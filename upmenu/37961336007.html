Dunkin’ Donuts adds nearly 600 stores in 2010

Patriot Ledger Staff

More of America was running on Dunkin’ in 2010, thanks to a successful effort to step up the pace for the Canton-based chain’s new store openings.

On Thursday, Dunkin’ Donuts said it enjoyed a net worldwide growth of 574 store locations in 2010, including 206 stores that opened in the United States. The chain now has more than 9,700 franchisee-owned restaurants in 31 countries.

The company also signed 226 new domestic franchise development agreements. Nigel Travis, the CEO of Dunkin’ Brands Inc., said that number represented a roughly 50 percent increase from 2009. Travis said that most of the new store openings and many of the development agreements came from existing franchise networks.

More than 450 Dunkin’ shops were remodeled last year to reflect the chain’s new store and brand design.

Looking forward to 2011, Dunkin’ is specifically targeting metro areas in the following states for growth: Wisconsin, Iowa, Arkansas, Missouri, Louisiana and Texas. The company has franchising seminars scheduled in Texas, Ohio and Alabama this month and next month.

In certain markets, the chain is offering development incentives to potential franchisees that include reduced royalty fees for three years and an extra $10,000 to be contributed for local in-store marketing.

Potential franchisees typically need to show they have a net worth of $500,000 and liquid assets of at least $250,000 to be considered by the company, although the precise requirements vary by region.
